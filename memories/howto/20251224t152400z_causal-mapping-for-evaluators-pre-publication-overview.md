- Pre-publication 2023 article on using causal mapping to support evaluators.
- Defines causal mapping as the collection, coding and visualization of interconnected causal claims to make provenance explicit and support evaluative judgments.
- Addresses the Janus dilemma: maps function as repositories of evidence about causal pathways, not only models of beliefs or causation.
- Distinguishes maps of group beliefs from individual maps; discusses aggregation, sensemaking, and relevance to the QuIP qualitative evaluation protocol.
- Traces history from 1970s to today, citing Axelrod, Eden, Laukkanen, Pearl; notes development of software/tools and guidelines.
- Reports action-research outcome: new software and guidelines for causal mapping used in evaluations worldwide.
- Emphasizes systematic use of causal mapping in evaluation beyond ad hoc applications.
