- Pre-publication article on causal mapping for evaluators by Powell, Copestake and Remnant.
- Explains causal mapping as collecting, coding and visualising causal claims; maps encode both beliefs and stakeholder views, serving as evidence for evaluative judgments.
- Discusses the Janus dilemma of causal maps and the role as repositories of evidence for pathways.
- Describes the article's aim to share experience and guidelines for evaluators and to report development of software/tools (QuIP protocol mentioned).
- Provides a brief history of causal mapping from the 1970s, with key contributors and evolving methods; highlights major milestones and software like Decision Explorer and CMAP3.
- Outlines evaluative tasks: data gathering, coding causal claims, answering evaluative questions; discusses strengths/weaknesses and future potential.
- Notes the article's global use in evaluations and its contribution to methodological discourse.
