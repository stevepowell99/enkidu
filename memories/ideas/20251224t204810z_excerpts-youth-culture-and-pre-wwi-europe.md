- Snapshot of cultural atmosphere in late 19th / early 20th century Europe: an aging Austrian state vs. a youthful artistic renewal (Rilke, Hofmannsthal, Stefan George, Hauptmann).
- Youth recognized shifts in artistic outlooks before institutions did; young figures acted as catalysts for wider generational change.
- Social and personal freedoms surged (sport, travel, disappearance of old markers like beards); a sense of regained spontaneity and experimentation is emphasized.
- The author links the eruption of WWI to an "excess of force" and internal dynamism rather than clear rational causes; war seen as a tragic discharge of accumulated energy.
- Notes on pacifism (Berta von Suttner, Nobel prize origin) and the fleeting wartime feeling of brotherhood contrasted with postwar xenophobic cultural bans and politicization of art.

Key quotes:
- "Österreich war ein alter Staat, von einem greisen Kaiser beherrscht..."
- "Jugend besitzt wie gewisse Tiere einen ausgezeichneten Instinkt für Witterungsumschläge..."
- "man heute ruhig überlegend sich fragt, warum Europa 1914 in den Krieg ging, findet man keinen einzigen Grund vernünftiger Art..."
